Liturgy:
(Acts XXVIII, 1-31)
Acts XXVIII, 4 — 'κρεμάμενον τὸ θηρίον ἐκ τῆς χειρὸς αὐτοῦ' — 'kremamenon to therion ek tes kheiros autou' - the dangerous creature hanging from his hand. Whence. 'διασωθέντα ἐκ τῆς θαλάσσης' - 'diasothenta ek tes thalasses' - saved from the sea. Whence. The preposition 'ek' in both these cases indicates whence the events and actions took place — without additional specification (that is, in general terms, as it is frequently used).

Acts XXVIII, 5 — 'εἰς τὸ πῦρ' — 'eis to pyr' - into the fire. Whither.

Acts XXVIII, 6 — 'εἰς αὐτὸν' — 'eis auton' - concerning him; with regard to him. With respect to whom. The preposition 'eis' indicates here with respect to whom the natives expected misfortune.

Acts XXVIII, 7 — 'ἐν δὲ τοῖς περὶ τὸν τόπον' — 'en de tois peri ton topon' - in the area around that place. Where. The prepositions 'en' together with 'peri' here form part of a common expression.

Acts XXVIII, 9, 11 — 'ἐν τῇ νήσῳ' — 'en te neso' - on the island. Where. The second translation variant emphasises the location rather than the placement.

Acts XXVIII, 11 — 'ἐν πλοίῳ' — 'en ploio' - in a ship; on a ship. Where.

Acts XXVIII, 12 — 'εἰς Συρακούσας' — 'eis Syrakousas' - into Syracuse. Whither.

Acts XXVIII, 13 — 'εἰς Ῥήγιον' — 'eis Rhegion' - into Rhegium; to Rhegium. Whither. 'εἰς Ποτιόλους' - 'eis Potiolous' - to Puteoli. Whither.

Acts XXVIII, 14, 16 — 'παρ' αὐτοῖς' — 'par autois' - in the vicinity of them; near them. The preposition 'para' in its form before the following vowel here with the dative case indicates that those brothers wished to be their close friends. 'εἰς τὴν Ῥώμην' - 'eis ten Rhomen' - into Rome; to Rome. Whither.

Acts XXVIII, 15 — 'εἰς ἀπάντησιν' — 'eis anantesin' - for a friendly meeting. The preposition 'eis' indicates here the purpose.

Acts XXVIII, 17 — 'δέσμιος ἐξ Ἱεροσολύμων παρεδόθην εἰς τὰς χεῖρας τῶν Ῥωμαίων' — 'desmios ex Hierosolymon paredothen eis ta kheiras ton Rhomaion' - a prisoner from Jerusalem delivered into the hands of the Romans. The pair of prepositions 'ek'-'eis' in their respective forms indicates here movement from and to — that is, whence and whither.

Acts XXVIII, 18 — 'ἐν ἐμοί' — 'en emoi' - in me.

Acts XXVIII, 21 — 'ἀπὸ τῆς Ἰουδαίας' — 'apo tes Ioudaias' - from Judaea. The preposition 'apo' indicates here that those possible (but in reality absent) letters would be from an entirely different region — having fully departed from it in order to be delivered to Rome.

Acts XXVIII, 22 — 'παρὰ σοῦ' — 'para sou' - from beside you; from you. The preposition 'para' here with the genitive case. This construction is used concerning the procession of the Holy Spirit from the Father, also concerning the entire Trinity, the entire God's design, and also concerning the human nature of Jesus Christ. See more at the links:
https://oleksandr-zhabenko.github.io/en/commentaries/Pryjmennyky.html

https://oleksandr-zhabenko.github.io/en/commentaries/28052026.html
Here it has a double meaning. The Jews trust Paul not because they are of one mind with him, but because he is a witness of those events to a considerably greater degree than they are. Therefore here it indicates that the Jews expect the action of the Holy Spirit through Paul — although not all are ready to accept his faith concerning Jesus. For those who are ready, the words of Paul will indeed confirm their expectations and faith, whilst for the rest Paul remained only to a certain degree a participant in the events. It is a pointer to the fact that true testimony also occurs with the participation of the Holy Spirit.

Acts XXVIII, 23 — 'εἰς τὴν ξενίαν' — 'eis ten xenian' - into the lodging (place for receiving guests and visitors). Whither. 'ἀπό τε τοῦ νόμου Μωϋσέως καὶ τῶν προφητῶν' - 'apo te tou nomou Moyseos kai ton prophetōn' - from the Law of Moses and the Prophets. The preposition 'apo' indicates here that those researches and words may be received objectively — that is, what is spoken of is a balanced interpretation of Holy Scripture, a correct understanding. 'ἀπὸ πρωῒ ἕως ἑσπέρας' - 'apo proi heos hesperas' - from morning until evening. The preposition 'apo' indicates here the starting point of the time reckoning within a certain interval.

Acts XXVIII, 30 — 'ἐν ἰδίῳ μισθώματι' — 'en idio misthomati' - in his own rented dwelling. That is, in a house he was renting. Where.

Acts XXVIII, 31 — 'παρρησιας' — 'parresias' — full utterance, freedom, boldness, confidence, openness, frankness.

The conclusion of the Acts of the Holy Apostles.

Paul and his companions visit Malta. The miracle with the serpent, the healing of the ruler and many of the inhabitants. The Maltese were at that time devout pagans — they treated the apostle Paul with great reverence after the miracles, and before that with great hospitality.

The further journey to Rome and the preaching among the Jews there is also described.
