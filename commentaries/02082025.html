In general in the text of Scripture there are 4 prepositions which can be translated in Ukrainian, Russian, Church Slavonic and other languages approximately as the Ukrainian from: 'ek', 'para', 'hypo' and 'apo'. Moreover the conducted analysis shows that the first two emphasise deep origin, which is fundamental, essential etc., the fourth emphasises separation, detachment, exit, and the third, which precisely stands in this place â€” Romans XIII, 1 â€” has OTHER meanings and therefore is used instead of all other 3.

The preposition 'hypo' in its various forms can be used with different cases, here with the genitive. Most often this indicates the passive state of the related verbal form, that is that that (the one, those) about which or about whom it is spoken, grammatically are not subjects of the action or state (who themselves perform it), but are objects of the action or state. In Scripture precisely such usage of such combination is the most common and typical. Therefore here also one can translate precisely so, since here also is the passive state â€” are established. Then the translation grammatically should be are established by God and emphasises the passive state of authorities in relation to God. God established authorities, not they themselves. One can say otherwise, that concerning God's action in this, human actions are completely insignificant, powerless, passive, while God is active. This means that precisely God determined that authorities as such will exist, what are their functions, forms, types, what commandments they should fulfil etc. Not authorities (particularly not people who have authority or struggle for it) determine all this, but this is given and determined by God, and human efforts, however they might be, cannot change or do anything here. That is, it concerns authority as such, as a phenomenon. It also concerns God's determination â€” if God determined something, then so it will be, regardless of the desire or action of anyone else. But this does not mean that literally all concrete realisations of authority, all concrete authorities (state, national and others) are determined by God in this sense.
