(Ephesians V, 20-26)
Ephesians V, 20 — ‘ἐν ὀνόματι’ - ‘en onomati’ - ‘in the name; by the name’. Rather the former than the latter, since it is not clear how to do the latter.

Ephesians V, 21 — ‘ἐν φόβῳ Χριστοῦ’ - ‘en phobo CHristou’ - ‘in the fear of Christ’. The ‘fear of Christ’ is the same as the ‘fear of God’, about which more is available here:
https://www.facebook.com/share/p/2oB4ATB64NBs6kCp/

Ephesians V, 24 — ‘ἐν παντί’ - ‘en panti’ - ‘in everything’.

Ephesians V, 26 — ‘ἐν ῥήματι’ - ‘en remati’ - ‘in the word; in the matter; by the word; by the matter’. A word and in general an expression with many meanings, but it is definitely not just about words, but about the essence of the matter.

For more information about the reading from the Apostle, please refer to the link:

(Luke XI, 1-10)
Luke XI, 1 — ‘ἐν τῷ εἶναι’ - ‘en to einai’ - ‘in being’. That is, ‘when; during’. ‘ἐν τόπῳ’ - ‘en topo’ - ‘in a place’.

Luke XI, 2 — the words ‘ours Who is in heaven’ are not found in many manuscripts.

Luke XI, 4 — ‘εἰς πειρασμόν’ - ‘eis peirasmon’ - ‘into temptation; into testing’. In the ancient Greek language, ‘temptation’ and ‘trial’ are the same word.

Luke XI, 5 — ‘ἐξ ὑμῶν’ - ‘ex ymon’ - ‘of you’. The preposition ‘ek’ in its form before the next vowel indicates where the choice is made from.

Luke XI, 6 — ‘ἐξ ὁδοῦ’ - ‘ex odou’ - ‘from the road’. That is, ‘from where?’.

Luke XI, 7 — ‘εἰς τὴν κοίτην’ - ‘eis ten koiten’ - ‘in bed’.
