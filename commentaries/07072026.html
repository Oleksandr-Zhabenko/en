Liturgy:
(1 Corinthians I, 1-9)
1 Corinthians I, 2, 3, 4, 7-9 — 'τῇ ἐκκλησίᾳ τοῦ θεοῦ τῇ οὔσῃ ἐν Κορίνθῳ' — 'te ekklesia tou theou te ouse en Korintho' - to the Church of God which is in Corinth. Where. 'ἐν Χριστῷ Ἰησοῦ' - 'en CHristo Iesou' - in Christ Jesus. People became called through fellowship with Christ. See more at the link:
https://gemini.google.com/share/026292afadd4?skid=c2bcfcbf-1516-4fdb-acbc-466068a9d0ea
'ἐν παντὶ τόπῳ' — 'en panti topo' - in every place. Where.

1 Corinthians I, 3 — 'ἀπὸ θεοῦ πατρὸς' — 'apo theou patros' - from God the Father. The preposition 'apo' emphasizes that this is God's gift.

1 Corinthians I, 4 — 'ἐν Χριστῷ Ἰησοῦ' — 'en CHristo Iesou' - in Christ Jesus. Christ is the Source of grace, and people through Christ become its partakers. See more at the link:
https://gemini.google.com/share/026292afadd4?skid=c2bcfcbf-1516-4fdb-acbc-466068a9d0ea

1 Corinthians I, 5 — 'ἐν παντὶ ἐπλουτίσθητε ἐν αὐτῷ, ἐν παντὶ λόγῳ καὶ πάσῃ γνώσει' — 'en panti eploutisthete en auto en panti logo kai pase gnosei' - in everything you became rich in Him, in every word and all knowledge. Here primarily it concerns spiritual gifts, but also material ones are not excluded (for example, wonderfully preserved and multiplied nature).

1 Corinthians I, 6 — 'ἐν ὑμῖν' — 'en hymin' - in you; among you. Where, among whom.

1 Corinthians I, 7 — 'ἐν μηδενὶ χαρίσματι' — 'en medeni kharismati' - in no gift of grace; in no grace. It is meant that by grace is named not only Divine energy, but also every benefit of God, see also above verse 5.

1 Corinthians I, 8 — 'ἐν τῇ ἡμέрᾳ τοῦ κυρίου ἡμῶν Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ' — 'en te hemera tou kyriou hemon Iesou CHristou' - in the Day of our Lord Jesus Christ. When. In general it concerns primarily the Second Glorious Coming of Christ, which also is a reminder also about the resurrection of the dead, God's judgement and the Kingdom.

1 Corinthians I, 9 — 'εἰς κοινωνίαν' — 'eis koinonian' - into fellowship; unto a community; into communion. Polysemantic words with different deep meanings. Evidently, the apostle had previously to explain in detail to the Corinthians the content of these words, which is why here he only reminds them about what they already know away from him and others. (Matthew XIII, 24-30)
Matthew XIII, 24 — 'ἐν τῷ ἀγρῷ' — 'en to agro' - in the field. Where.

Matthew XIII, 25 — 'ἐν δὲ τῷ καθεύδειν' — 'en de to katheudein' - but when [they] slept; but during sleep. When.

Matthew XIII, 27 — 'ἐν τῷ σῷ ἀγρῷ' — 'en to so agro' - in your field. Where.

Matthew XIII, 30 — 'ἐν καιρῷ τοῦ θερισμοῦ' — 'en kairo tou therismou' - in the time of harvest. When. 'εἰς δέσμας' - 'eis desmas' - into bundles. The preposition 'eis' indicates here onto how they shall do with the tares (a change of state). 'εἰς τὴν ἀποθήκην' - 'eis ten apotheken' - into the barn; into the granary. Where to.

Venerable Job and Theodosius of Manyava:
(Galatians V, 22-VI, 2; Luke VI, 17-23)
The fruits of the Holy Spirit are nine, and I think that they can be grouped by three into groups, where they mutually correlate between the groups in the same manner. This reminds of the Revelation of the Trinity: the Father is manifested through the Son in the Holy Spirit. The first virtue in each triad of the list is the root out of which is manifested the second, and through it the third. See:

1. if anyone lives in love, then joy develops, is granted, is manifested in this person, and into them comes heavenly peace;

2. if anyone is in longsuffering (willing endurance, good steadfastness), then develops gentleness [kindness] (Christ-like excellence, virtue), and comes into the heart and life goodness (heartfelt warmth, mercy, compassion, etc.);

3. if anyone lives in faith, then develops meekness (krotost in Slavonic, that is, non-wrathfulness, mastery over one's passions, the skill and state of not provoking oneself and others) and comes temperance — self-control, possession of oneself.

For such there is no law — that is, the Law is for the transgressors of the Law, and for such people operates God's grace, they live according to it. Also such triads can be distinguished out of Paul's hymn of love, and the three theological virtues — love, hope, and faith (longsuffering is directly connected with hope, for without hope it is impossible). Therefore, love,.., hope,.., faith,..

And living so, we can live according to Christ's love.
