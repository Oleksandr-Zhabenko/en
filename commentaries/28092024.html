(1 Corinthians IV, 1-5)
1 Corinthians IV, 2 — ‘ἐν τοῖς οἰκονόμοις’ - ‘en tois oikonomois’ - ‘in the householders’.

1 Corinthians IV, 3 — ‘εἰς ἐλάχιστόν’ - ‘eis elakhiston’ - ‘in the least’. That is, it becomes the least significant, loses its meaning down to almost nothing. ‘ὑφ’ ὑμῶν - ‘yph ymon’ - ‘under you; through you’. ‘ὑπὸ ἀνθρωπίνης ἡμέρας’ - ‘ypo anthropines emeras’ - ‘under human judgement; through human judgement’. The preposition ‘ypo’ with the genitive case.

1 Corinthians IV, 4 — ‘ἐν τούτῳ’ - ‘en touto’ - ‘in this; by this’. Both translations are possible, although they are different.

1 Corinthians IV, 5 — ‘ἀπὸ τοῦ θεοῦ’ - ‘apo tou theou’ - ‘from God’. The preposition ‘apo’ indicates that God will be generous even in judgement and that there will be something evangelical, heavenly in the life of each person for which God will praise.

(Luke V, 17-26)
Luke V, 17 — ‘ἐν μιᾷ τῶν ἡμερῶν’ - ‘en mia ton emeron’ - ‘on one of the days; on one day’. ‘ἐκ πάσης κώμης’ - ‘ek pases komes’ - ‘from all the villages’. ‘εἰς τὸ ἰᾶσθαι’ - ‘eis to iasthai’ - ‘in healing; for healing’.

Luke V, 19 — ‘εἰς τὸ μέσον ἔμπροσθεν’ - ‘eis to meson emprosthen’ - ‘’inside before‘’.

Luke V, 22 — ‘ἐν ταῖς καρδίαις’ - ‘en tais kardiais’ - ‘in the hearts’.

Luke V, 24, 25 — ‘εἰς τὸν οἶκόν’ - ‘eis ton oikon’ - ‘into the house’.
