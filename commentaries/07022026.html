Exposure of hypocrisy, and also praise of the poor widow and her modest gift of love and poverty. A story which often inspired people to monasticism, in particular to voluntary poverty for the sake of drawing near to God. A well-known thought about that God looks first of all upon our hearts, in particular also concerning the value of offerings and gifts.

Both parts are united by one thought â€” if the person does something which is connected with faith not by virtue of a sincere heart, but in view of other reasons, for example tradition, social structure, level of social development and so on, then this does not make him truly righteous, and can also occupy the holy place of true relations with God and people, make the person almost incorrigible and tempt others to that.
