Liturgy:
(Romans XII, 6-14)
Romans XII, 6 — 'εἴτε προφητείαν, κατὰ τὴν ἀναλογίαν τῆς πίστεως' — 'eite propheteian kata ten analogian tes pisteos' - if prophecy, according to the proportion [measure] of faith. An important detail — the word proportion (measure), and not simply faith. Its addition must change the meaning of what is said, otherwise there would be no sense in adding an extra word to the frequent expression according to faith. It concerns prophecies from contemporary Christians (with respect to the addressees of the Epistle, but in view of the Word of God — for those contemporary to the reader, as well as the previous ones). Here it is not about the recognized Old Testament prophets nor about John the Baptist. That is, it speaks about private revelations and the attitude towards them. Paul's words do not reject them, but prompt to receive them in the measure of the grace of faith. That is, if in some revelation or prophecy one can see a manifest action of grace, then for this sake it is necessary to receive it and trust it in that measure. If, however, there is no manifest action of grace, then there is no need to trust it either. One way or another, such revelations must not contradict faith itself, Christ, Who must be received wholly, completely, not in measure, but unconditionally.

Romans XII, 7 — 'ἐν τῇ διακονίᾳ' — 'en te diakonia' - in serving [in ministry]. 'ἐν τῇ διδασκαλίᾳ' - 'en te didaskalia' - in teaching. In what activity.

Romans XII, 8 — 'ἐν τῇ παραкλήσει' — 'en te paraklesei' - in exhortation [in consolation]. 'ἐν ἁπλότητι' - 'en haploteti' - in simplicity, sincerity, purity. 'ἐν σπουδῇ' - 'en spoude' - in diligence., 'ἐν ἱλαρότητι' - 'en hilaroteti' - in cheerfulness, in joyfulness. These two verses emphasize the constancy of the indicated qualities and states, as if at home, their organic nature for those types of ministry and activity.

Romans XII, 10 — 'εἰς ἀλλήλους' — 'eis allelous' - into one another [toward one another]. Here the direction and mobility of love are emphasized — the motivating power of love for actions.

I wrote a little earlier, to quote:
The apostle teaches how to interact with God's gifts.

I will dwell on one phrase from the first — he who gives — in simplicity (sincerely, with a single heart, non-double-minded) (Romans XII, 8 ).

It is important to remember that sin often does not want to retreat, and even tries to use good deeds, thoughts, feelings for itself. Therefore, it is good to give, as the Lord witnesses through the lips of Paul, but an extraordinary necessity of Christian giving is to do so with the whole heart, non-double-minded, sincerely, disinterestedly. Much like Christian ruling is true leadership that inspires and gives vigor.

End of quote.

(Matthew IX, 1-8 )
Matthew IX, 1 — 'εἰς πλοῖον' — 'eis ploion' - into a boat. It means some boat, possibly someone out of His non-closest disciples invited Him. 'εἰς τὴν ἰδίαν πόλιν' - 'eis ten idian polin' - into His own city.

Matthew IX, 3 — 'ἐν ἑαυτοῖς' — 'en heautois' - in themselves; within themselves. Where. It concerns internal speech (mentally, soundlessly) (see also verse 4).

Matthew IX, 4 — 'ἐν ταῖς καρδίαις' — 'en tais kardiais' - in the hearts. Where.

Matthew IX, 6, 7 — 'εἰς τὸν οἶκόν' — 'eis ton oikon' - into the house. Where to.

The healing of the paralytic by the faith of his companions, also the forgiveness of sins. Here the Lord forgives sins — by the faith of the companions (neighbors) for the glory of God and for the testimony about Himself and about God. That is, the visible goal of this also is God's glory.

We are often accustomed to see in the forgiveness of sins primarily God's mercy and love, but in Scripture we can meet the forgiveness of sins as a manifestation of God's righteousness (justice) (see:
https://oleksandr-zhabenko.github.io/en/commentaries/05022026.html
), and here — of God's glory. But these reasons do not deny also God's love and mercy, they presuppose them.

Also, it is important to note that the forgiveness of sins took place by the faith of other people.
