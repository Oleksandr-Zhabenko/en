I note that animals, like plants, are created according to their kind, and human – in the image of God. No matter what specific meaning you put into these words (for example, under image of God you can understand the immortal soul, you can understand that every person is a personality, you can have freedom and reason, you can have something similar), but this emphasizes the difference in the origin of human and animals with plants.

Ours – emphasizes that God is the Holy Trinity – the Father, the Son and the Holy Spirit. By the way, the word God in the Old Testament is very often in the plural – Elohim, where im indicates the plural, and the verbs relating to God are singular, which emphasizes the unity of the Holy Trinity (like Gods created).

The 1st chapter of the book of Genesis fits human into the general context of the creation of the whole world, while the 2nd chapter is addressed to human himself / herself as a unique being. If in the 1st chapter human appears as a special part of nature, creation in general, then in the 2nd we see his / her relationship with God and more specifics. In the 1st chapter, the participation of the Trinity, dialogue and the appeal of God to human are outlined, then in the 2nd we see a picture of relationships. This may be due to the simple logic of the narrative, where depth increases as the sequel progresses, first introduction, then story and relationships. But it can also be an important indication and shed light on who a person is. This can mean that a person reveals himself / herself fully in his / her relationship with God and in his / her personal actions and communication. This can help to understand, in fact, the entire history of the creation of the world.

Male and female created He them – the equality in nature of men and women, boys and girls, their only and common nature – human is emphasized.

There are many questions that need to be answered, and therefore search and interest exist. For example, God did not create death, but even in Paradise, before the fall of people, the food for people and animals was herbs and fruits, and this consumption was not called death, but was an organic component of life.

Another interesting question: is the order of dominion over the animals in God’s blessing important or not? Does it mean that we need to start paying more attention to marine animals (fish), then birds, then land animals, then those that live in land (soil) and in mixed conditions? Is this order simply the reverse of the order of their creation by the Word of God – and it only means that God brought all animals to humans in LIFO / FILO order – the last to be created, because they were closer to humans (perhaps they did not have time to go far), were brought first, and the first were already further – and therefore the last? In general, should it be understood as a scientific material or as a semantic description (for example, Reverend Maksym the Confessor, the father of the Church, understood that the first thing that is said here is not about the creation itself, but more about God’s designs (seed logos, ideas) regarding it and how these designs then interacted with matter)? Is this the organic unity of various layers of understanding described in the first link above?

For example, in relation to the seventh day of creation, the organic integral unity of meanings is provided by the text. According to the understanding of the fathers, the seventh day of creation continues until now and will continue until the Second Coming, when the eighth and eternal day of creation will come, while God (in particular, the Father) continues to provide and act in creation. At the same time, this very structure of creation caused the appearance of the 4th commandment regarding the Sabbath – the seventh day of rest.
