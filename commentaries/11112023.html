The Apostle Paul here praises the Macedonians to the Corinthians, in particular the Thessalonians, the letter to whom we read this week (also the Philippians), and holds them up as an example of generosity beyond their means, generosity that is deeply sacrificial, selfless, with a complete surrender of themselves to God and a manifestation of faithfulness to the apostles and the other churches.

Paul emphasises that such sacrificial generosity comes from God's grace, or rather, from its abundance and richness, and rejoices with them. We need to remember that all good things are given to people, including the faithful, through the work of God's grace, but not every gift of God is grace itself. Nevertheless, as I wrote earlier, the word grace is used at least sometimes in the New Testament in a broad sense, referring to various gifts of God, not necessarily the divine energy that is most often referred to by this word in the New Testament.
