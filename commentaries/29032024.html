For Vespers:
(Genesis V, 32-VI, 8)
Genesis VI, 6 — 'wayyinnahem' - And he sighed in sorrow - from 'nacham' - to sigh; to regret; to pity; to console. The translation is often unfortunate - regretted, repented - unfortunate because in reality God does not repent because He does not do anything wrong. God was grieved by the constant evil and focus on the evil of people, in fact, by their incorrigibility and unrepentance, which leads to destruction, but He did not react angrily, but providentially, saving Noah and his family, preventing evil from outliving Noah and remaining the only meaning of people's lives.

The important point is that it is not so much about people's deeds as about people's hearts, and the Lord is the only One Who knows people's hearts and can judge them.
