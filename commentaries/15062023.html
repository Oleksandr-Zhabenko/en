Reconciled – literally changed mutually, came to a compromise, exchanged. It means that Christ died in our place (exchange), and this made people and God alike in Christ (brought them closer together, became like a compromise). The meaning of reconciliation is derived from the general meaning of the passage. The Sacrifice of the Son of God became the Sacrifice of reconciliation.

Romans V, 12 is a difficult passage to understand and translate. A more accurate translation might be death passed in that all have sinned. That is, sin is the conductor of death, figuratively speaking, the channel through which death came. But the difficulty arises when we compare it with verse 14, in which the translation roughly gives: from Adam to Moses, even over (direction of distribution - where?) those who did not sin in (presence in, state in) the likeness of Adamâ€™s transgression. We can conclude from these two verses that sin became the conduit and the doorway to death, but then death does not exist in itself in full accordance with sin. That is, it has spread (or at least is capable of spreading) to those who have not sinned (the most vivid example is Jesus Christ).

Paul goes on to speak of the gift of grace through the Resurrection of Jesus Christ in comparison to the fall of Adam. This gift is much greater because it covers many trespasses and sins.
