Liturgy:
(Acts XVIII, 22-28)
Acts XVIII, 22 — 'κατελθὼν εἰς Καισάριαν' — 'katelthon eis Kaisarian' - having gone down to Caesarea. Whither. 'κατέβη εἰς Ἀντιόχειαν' - 'katebe eis Antiokheian' - he went down to Antioch. Whither.

Acts XVIII, 24 — 'εἰς Ἔφεσον' — 'eis Epheson' - into Ephesus. Whither. 'δυνατὸς ὢν ἐν ταῖς γραφαῖς' - 'dynatos on en tais graphais' - being strong in the Scriptures. In what he was strong.

Acts XVIII, 26 — 'παρρησιάζεσθαι' — 'parresiazesthai' — he conducted himself and spoke confidently and openly. 'ἐν τῇ συναγωγῇ' — 'en te synagoge' - in the synagogue. Where. Priscilla — a Latin feminine name — is the wife of Aquila. The wife is named before the husband, owing to her greater activity in furthering the preaching and the spreading of the faith.

Acts XVIII, 27 — 'εἰς τὴν Ἀχαΐαν' — 'eis ten Akhaian' - into Achaia. Whither. 'προτρεψάμενοι' — 'protrepsamenoi' — literally: they turned them forward towards themselves — that is, they encouraged them; the English 'exhorting'; possibly also 'encouraging'.

Acts XVIII, 28 — 'δημοσίᾳ' — 'demosia' — publicly, openly.

The story of the joining of the Jew Apollos to the Christians. The meaning of his characterisation is that he taught a good and devout life — in much of its content similar in many ways to how Jesus did this — but on the basis of repentance. In effect this means that he correctly understood the preaching of John the Baptist, but did not know about Jesus. When they told him about Jesus, he continued already as a Christian (he is a Saint and one of the seventy Apostles).

Paul also mentions him in his Epistles.

The story is also about God's calling of a person from the side.
