(1 Timothy I, 8-14)
1 Timothy I, 12, 14 - 'ἡμῶν' - 'emon' - 'us' (our).

The apostle Paul testifies that the Law of Moses is good if it is used correctly - first of all, to fight against sin, to expose it, to know what sin is. The Law does not write what is truly righteousness, but rather exposes sin so that one can see through it what is sin.

Paul goes on to testify that this understanding is from the Gospel, and that he himself became a minister of God through God's grace, having previously been ignorant, even a persecutor.

(Luke XIX, 45-48)
Luke XIX, 47 - 'ἡμέραν' - 'emeran' - 'day' (daily).

A quote is taken from the prophets Isaiah and Jeremiah.

There is a beautiful prayer about the house of prayer and the cave of thieves:
You generously give, O Lord, so grant us also,
that our hearts may be true houses of faithfulness, fidelity,
and not the caves of thieves.
