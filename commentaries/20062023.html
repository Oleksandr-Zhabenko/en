This is a difficult place, where Paul speaks rather on behalf of another person (to gain at least some, according to him, he himself is in a better state through the grace of the Lord). In this way, Paul wants his hearers (readers) to quicker agree with his deep thoughts.

In general, we can say that the flesh here does not refer to the actual component of human nature created by God, but to the state of corruption of human nature (like the skins of animals that the Lord gave Adam and Eve to put on when they were expelled from Paradise because of the fall and unrepentance).
