In addition to the words mentioned in the references, the following are also included:

John XIII, 31, XVII, 14, 16, XVIII, 5, 6, 8, 17, 25, 35, 37, Matthew XXVII, 24 — ‘εἰμι’ - ‘eimi’ - (I) ‘am’. It is used for emphasis.

John XIII, 35, XV, 8, XVIII, 35, 36 — ‘ἐμοὶ’ - ‘emoi’ - ‘Me’ (My).

John XV, 9 — ‘ἐμῇ’ - ‘eme’ - ‘to My’.

John XV, 11, 12, XVIII, 36 — ‘ἐμὴ’ - ‘eme’ - ‘My’ (Mine).

John XVI, 14, XVIII, 8, Luke XXIII, 43 — ‘ἐμὲ’ - ‘eme’ - ‘Me’. ‘ἐμοῦ’ - ‘emou’ - ‘Me’ (My; by Me).

John XVII, 14 — ‘ἐμίσησεν’ - ‘emisesen’ - ‘hated’ - from ‘μισέω’ - ‘miseo’ - ‘to hate; to detest’.

John XVIII, 27 — ‘ἀλέκτωρ’ - ‘alektor’ - ‘cock’.

John XVIII, 32 — ‘ἤμελλεν’ - ‘emellen’ - ‘was about to’ - from ‘μέλλω’ - ‘mello’ - ‘to be about to’.

John XIX, 1 — ‘ἐμαστίγωσεν’ - ‘emastigosen’ - ‘to flog’ - from ‘μαστιγόω’ - ‘mastigoo’ - ‘to flog; to scourge’.

Matthew XXVII, 4, 25 — ‘ἥμαρτον’ - ‘emarton’ - ‘sinned’ - from ‘ἁμαρτάνω’ - ‘amartano’ - ‘to sin’. ‘ἡμᾶς’ - ‘emas’ - ‘us’. ‘ἡμῶν’ - ‘emon’ - ‘us’ (our).

Matthew XXVII, 28 — ‘κοκκίνην’ - ‘kokkinen’ - ‘crimson’.

Matthew XXVII, 30 — ‘ἐμπτύσαντες’ - ‘emptysantes’ - ‘spitting upon’ - from ‘ἐμπτύω’ - ‘emptyo’ - ‘to spit upon’.

Mark XV, 29 — ‘ἡμέραις’ - ‘emerais’ - ‘to the days’ (days).

Mark XV, 30 — ‘ἐμπαίζοντες’ - ‘empaizontes’ - ‘mocking’ - from ‘ἐμπαίζω’ - ‘empaizo’ - ‘to mock’.

Luke XXIII, 39 — ‘ἡμᾶς’ - ‘emas’ - ‘us’.

Luke XXIII, 41 — ‘ἡμεῖς’ - ‘emeis’ - ‘we’.

John XIII, 38 — ‘ἐμοῦ’ - ‘emou’ - ‘Me’.

John XVIII, 34 — ‘ἐμοῦ’ - ‘emou’ - ‘Me’.

John XVIII, 31 — ‘ἡμῖν’ - ‘emin’ - ‘to us’.

Matthew XXVII, 11, 29 — ‘ἔμπροσθεν’ - ‘emprosthen’ - ‘before; in the face’.
