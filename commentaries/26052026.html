Liturgy:
(Acts XXI, 26-32)
Acts XXI, 26, 28, 29 — 'εἰς τὸ ἱερόν' — 'eis to hieron' - into the temple. Whither.

Acts XXI, 27 — 'οἱ ἀπὸ τῆς Ἀσίας Ἰουδαῖοι' — 'hoi apo tes Asias Ioudaioi' - the Jews from Asia. The preposition 'apo' indicates here that they had completely left Asia — had resettled. 'ἐν τῷ ἱερῷ' - 'en to hiero' - in the temple. Where.

Acts XXI, 29 — 'ἐν τῇ πόλει' — 'en te polei' - in the city. Where.

Paul, having heeded the advice of the apostle James, the brother of the Lord and first bishop of Jerusalem, performed in the Jerusalem temple a devout Jewish (Old Testament) rite of purification — but people, stirred up by enemies of Paul and of the Christians and excited by false rumours, almost carried out a lynching there against him. Only the intervention of the authorities and the police of that time stopped the crowd. The accusations are entirely unfounded, founded on the intolerance of the Jews towards Christians.

We see the intervention of the police by God's will in the matter for the protection of Paul and the continuation of his apostolic work. In general this is the direct beginning of the last part of the book of Acts, which recounts the trial of Paul and his ministry therein, and God's help and furtherance for him.

(John XVI, 2-13)
John XVI, 4 — 'ἐξ ἀρχῆς' — 'ex arkhes' - from the beginning. Common expression. The preposition 'ek' in its form before the following vowel indicates here in terms of meaning a greater communion with Christ (the emphasis) — that is, closeness not in time but in life. It is implied that Christ reckons the time here from the moment the apostles became like Him in their way of life, rather than from the beginning of their discipleship [this sentence is translated by Google Gemini Pro].

John XVI, 5 — 'οὐδεὶς ἐξ ὑμῶν' — 'oudeis ex hymon' - none of you.

John XVI, 9 — 'οὐ πιστεύουσιν εἰς ἐμέ' — 'ou pisteuousin eis eme' - they do not believe in(to) Me. See at the links:

John XVI, 13 — 'ὁδηγήσει ὑμᾶς ἐν τῇ ἀληθείᾳ πάσῃ' — 'odegesei hymas en te aletheia pase' - He will lead (direct, show the way to) you in all truth; He will lead (direct, show the way to) you with all truth. Both translations are possible and complement one another. It must be remembered that the knowledge of truth is always a gift of grace — and moreover, in various cases (in certain senses, in particular concerning Christ, the Father, and the Holy Spirit, as also here in the text) this gift even surpasses the gift of salvation, since a person may be saved yet may be in error. 'οὐ γὰρ λαλήσει ἀφ' ἑαυτοῦ' - 'ou gar lalesei aph heautou' - for He will not speak from Himself. See the detailed explanation below.

John XVI, 13 — certainly the most eloquent testimony in the Bible to the fact that the Holy Spirit is a Person, a Personality, a Hypostasis. For He is not only able to relay what He has heard but can also speak both from Himself and not from Himself (in reality the latter — but the very negation of the former also declares that He is Himself!). Those who assert that the Holy Spirit is a divine power rather than a Person are in error — for this verse (and not only this one, but this one most expressly) exposes the incorrectness of such an understanding of the Holy Spirit. No — in truth the Holy Spirit is a Person, is a Hypostasis. And He will testify not as evidence, but as a Living True Witness. And wherein is the most notable difference between the testimony of evidence and the testimony of living rational witnesses?

Evidence testifies through its interpretation — which is not given by the evidence itself but by people to it — whilst living rational witnesses themselves interpret and thereby testify, and can also correct others' incorrect understandings and respond to objections. The Holy Spirit the Comforter will not only reveal all that Christ speaks of, but is Himself also the salvation, help, and strengthening of those who draw near to God, of those who repent and are converted.

Thereby the Holy Spirit is incomparably more significant than a mere means of salvation. No — He is God, the Lord, the Life-giver, as the Creed says. Truly by His action — and one may speak of the richness and diversity of His actions — He also accomplishes our salvation together with the Father and the Son.

I shall add that not only Christ testifies: Look upon Me, turn to Me! — but the Holy Spirit also testifies (and will testify): Look upon Christ, turn to Him! — as also the Father testifies: Look upon Christ, turn to Him! And as the Law says, the testimony of several witnesses is true.

In the original ancient Greek in John XVI, 13 we have: 'ἀλλ' ὅσα ἀκούει λαλήσει' — 'all hosa akouei lalesei' — …but as much as He hears, He will speak....
The Holy Spirit will not be a broken telephone (Google Gemini Pro notes [
https://g.co/gemini/share/3b25330f07f5
] that the game is known throughout the world under various names and is also widely used as a model for research and explanation). The Holy Spirit will not allow anything of the truth to be lost, garbled, or distorted.

This points not only to the factual accuracy of the Holy Spirit (who, what precisely, where, when, and so on) but also to mathematical accuracy (quantity, number, in general, relation — how much).

It also means that everything the Trinity, in communicating among Themselves, wishes to reveal — the Holy Spirit will reveal.

People can often misunderstand new information and new data — but the Holy Spirit will not allow matters to worsen against the truth in connection with the new.

It is evident here that theology will continue to develop — in particular in connection with new things that are coming. But at the same time this does not mean something entirely new that was wholly absent from what Christ has already revealed. Something new that manifests what was already revealed — something new that corresponds to what is new, yet already revealed.

The Holy Spirit will not only enable a correct response to what is new, but will also announce it beforehand, will prepare, will give a forecast, will reveal in advance, and will bestow correct expectations.

He will also set free.

In general, He will give everything that a person expects from the truth.
