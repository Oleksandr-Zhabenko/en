A parable about how a person can become captivated by the material, forgetting about God, and captivation with one's own material well-being can prevent a person even from helping other people. Then the death of such a person or the Second Coming of Jesus Christ will reveal that what the person unreasonably appropriated for themselves is in reality insufficient for eternity, and the material goods themselves will often be distributed not as would have pleased that person.

Verse 21 more precisely literally sounds like: so it is with the one who stores up treasures for themselves, and does not become rich towards (in the direction, as a point of destination, goal) God.

The generalised language about the one speaks precisely about the generality of the described situation ‚Äî it concerns all, and not only those with whom precisely such happened.

Jesus Christ urges to care first of all about life with God. Seek first the Kingdom of God, and everything else will be added to you, ‚Äî He says in another Gospel.
