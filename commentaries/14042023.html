(Romans V, 6-11) Reasoning about death for a good-doer, a righteous person and a sinner - a good-doer evokes sincere feelings, in particular gratitude, so you can die with more courage for him or her, a righteous person cares how to please God, therefore people do not particularly admire him or her, and sinners - about them among people just few think with love, let alone suffer for them, and then, when there were few Christians, almost no one thought except them.

(Hebrews X, 19-31) It speaks of the importance and significance of the ministry of Jesus, His Sacrifice, and also of the punishment for trampling His Sacrifice under foot (that is, deliberate unrepentant contempt).

(1 Corinthians I, 18 - II, 2) Very eloquent and expressive words, in my opinion. In addition, I will say that to boast means to recognize the dignity, merit, superiority of something or someone, as well as to advise others as good. That is, the words that the faithful mean about the cross of Jesus Christ do not mean that they are proud of it, consider themselves better than others, but that they see in it a great meaning, value, a source of strength and wisdom, a manifestation of great love, an instrument of victory over sins and death, the banner of victory, and other things that are difficult to understand for those who do not accept the truth about Jesus.
