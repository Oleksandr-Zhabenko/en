According to the above, in particular the references, Paul is writing about the origin of the life of the righteous - it is a continuation and manifestation of their faith. He is not saying that faith is the source of life in itself (as some unclear translations might suggest), but that the life of the righteous is the organic continuation and logical consequence of their faith, and at the same time is a movement towards the future fulfilment of faith as the expected Kingdom of God. Faith in these words is the starting point of life, and in the previous ones it is also the point of designation of movement, but already accomplished, that is, to a certain extent achieved and fulfilled. A person who has fulfilled God's will and commandments is righteous, and at the same time their state can be called new faith, in which they have arrived through fulfilment and life. At the beginning of the verse, the same phrase is used.
