Liturgy:
(Romans XV, 17-29)
Romans XV, 17 — 'ἐν Χριστῷ Ἰησοῦ' — 'en CHristo Iesou' - in Christ Jesus. Here it is meant that the boasting of Paul is a boasting, in the end, with unity with Christ.

Romans XV, 18 — 'εἰς ὑπακοὴν' — 'eis hypakoen' - into obedience; unto obedience; for obedience. 'ἐθνῶν' - 'ethnon' - of Gentiles; of nations; of ethnoses. The apostle meant, most likely of all, precisely the first meaning, but the Holy Spirit through Paul witnesses also about all three meanings. See also yesterday's reading and the commentaries at the link:
https://oleksandr-zhabenko.github.io/en/commentaries/01072026.html

Romans XV, 19 — 'ἐν δυνάμει' — 'en dynamei' - in power; with (by means of) power. Both meanings are possible. 'ἀπὸ Ἱερουσαλὴμ' - 'apo Ierousalem' - from Jerusalem. The preposition 'apo' emphasizes that Paul left Jerusalem and went further.

Verses 20-21 have an important significance for the question of a possible dispute with Peter. In my opinion, they testify about the absence of such in this Epistle (Paul did not try to win the disciples of Peter over to his side). See in more detail at the link:
https://oleksandr-zhabenko.github.io/en/commentaries/29062026.html

Romans XV, 23 — 'ἐν τοῖς κλίμασι τούτοις' — 'en tois klimasi toutois' - in these regions (territories). Where. 'ἀπὸ πολλῶν ἐτῶν' - 'apo pollon eton' - since many years, that is, since many years [for many years]. The preposition 'apo' emphasizes that this desire of Paul arose after certain important changes, in particular after he became an apostle.

Romans XV, 24 — 'εἰς τὴν Σπανίαν' — 'eis ten Spanian' - into Spain. Where to. 'ἀπὸ μέρους' - 'apo merous' - in part. A set expression.

Romans XV, 25 — 'εἰς Ἱερουσαλὴμ' — 'eis Hierousalem' - into Jerusalem. Where to.

Romans XV, 26 — 'εἰς τοὺς πτωχοὺς τῶν ἁγίων τῶν ἐν Ἱερουσαλήμ' — 'eis tous ptokhous ton hagion ton en Hierousalem' - for the poor of the saints who [are] in Jerusalem. Here the preposition 'eis' indicates onto the goal of the action, for the sake of what the offerings are.

Romans XV, 27 — ‘εκοινωνησαν’ — ‘ekoinonesan’ — shared, parted, had fellowship. 'ἔθνη' — 'ethne' - Gentiles; nations; ethnoses. Here it concerns precisely the Gentiles, former Gentiles, people of different nations. 'ἐν τοῖς σαρκικοῖς λειτουργῆσαι αὐτοῖς' - 'en tois sarkikois leitourgesai autois' - in the fleshly (matters) to act jointly to them. Analogously to yesterday's reading, the common deed is emphasized.

Romans XV, 28 — 'εἰς Σπανίαν' — 'eis Spanian' - into Spain. Where to.

Romans XV, 29 — 'ἐν πληρώμαти' — 'en pleromati' - in fullness. Here the instrumental case is not appropriate, therefore precisely (being) in fullness.

With a holy kiss — with that which does not provoke sinful temptations or passions. See at the link:
https://oleksandr-zhabenko.github.io/en/Kiss.html

In the XV chapter Paul speaks about his service and plans for the future (it is unknown whether Paul was in Spain, there are no testimonies about that, most likely of all, no). Worthy of attention is that the Christian community is not simply a spiritual union, but also takes an active part in the material needs of the faithful and of different people.

In the next chapter Paul completes the Epistle with personal greetings to chosen Christians, many of whom are saints (the majority in the rank of the 70 apostles).

(Matthew XII, 46-XIII, 3)
Matthew XII, 50 — 'τὸ θέλημα τοῦ πατρός μου τοῦ ἐν οὐραноῖς' — 'to thelema tou patros mou tou en ouranois' - the will of the Father of Me, the [One] in the heavens. Where. Here under the word will is meant precisely that which the Father desires.

Matthew XIII, 1 — 'Ἐν τῇ ἡμέρᾳ ἐκείνῃ' — 'en te hemera ekeine' - in that day. That is, during one day with the previous events. 'ἐκ τῆς οἰκίας' - 'ek tes oikias' - out of the house. Where from. 'παρὰ τὴν θάλασσαν' - 'para ten thalassan' - to the proximity with the sea. The preposition 'para' here is with the accusative and indicates here onto that Christ approached the sea shore.

Matthew XIII, 2 — 'εἰς πλοῖον' — 'eis ploion' - into a boat (to a boat). Where to.

Matthew XIII, 3 — 'ἐν παραβολαῖς' — 'en parabolais' - in parables; with (by means of) parables. Both variants of translation are correct.

A great gift of God is to be a relative to Jesus, or closely to know Him, as He Himself says: Blessed are you that see, — that which prophets, righteous people and kings wished to see, but did not see. Main, however, the most important — is the fulfillment of God's will. For this latter better unites with Christ than the presence of fleshly ties.
