Dedicated to Emma Kok.

(Acts VIII, 18-25)

Acts VIII:20 – ‘ενομισας’ – literally ‘to do according to the law (custom)’, meaning that he was not just thinking of getting God’s gift for money, but that was his usual way of thinking - ‘everything can be obtained for money, even God’s gifts’.

Acts VIII, 21 – literally, the heart is not straight (as opposed to crooked and twisted), i.e. it is precisely the twisted heart of this Simon that is being addressed.

Acts VIII, 22 – literally, if the attention of the mind of your heart is forgiven – that is, the intention in this case is called the attention (direction”) of the mind of the heart”.

Acts VIII, 24 – ‘επ’ εμε’ – (so that nothing of the like happens) over (to) me.

The story of simony, i.e. the purchase of spiritual gifts and privileges for money or material values. This is one of the most serious sins, and the name comes from the name of this Simon. ‘Simony’ is a resemblance to this Simon who wanted to receive the gifts of the Holy Spirit for money.

Why is the heart in simony not straight (right)? Because it implies taking God (God’s grace) for hostage to material circumstances and things – specifically, money. Such a conception of God simply puts Him on a par with entirely material things, and is therefore seriously wrong and blasphemous.

(John VI, 35-39)

John VI, 35 – ‘προς εμε’ – ‘pros eme’ – ‘to Me’, ‘εις εμε’ – ‘eis eme’ – ‘in Me’.

John VI, 37 – ‘προς εμε’ and ‘προς με’ – ‘to Me’.

John VI, 38 – ‘το θελημα το εμον’ – ‘My will’.

Christ speaks of His mission on land at the time of His First Coming. Briefly the sense of Christianity as such.

A person who comes to Christ is a person who is permanently moving, making certain efforts, towards Christ. That is, it is never an occasional process.

That is, if a person consciously tries and cares to get closer to Jesus Christ, He will not hinder him or her, will help him or her in this and will not shun or expel him or her from Him. The Lord is always waiting for our genuineness and ourselves.
