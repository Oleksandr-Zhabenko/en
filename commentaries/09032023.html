(Isaiah VI, 1-12)

Isaiah’s vision, the Lord of hosts, seraphim, purification of the prophet, God’s judgment.

A very mysterious, mystical place with many mysteries. However, there are many common opinions.

The description of seraphim is actually the only one in the Old Testament, they are considered the highest (at least of those known in the Scriptures) in an angelic way, who are closest to God, ablaze with God’s love, through them people can also rise to God in it.

In particular, the purification of Isaiah allows him to carry the ministry of the prophet.

We see his humility in the words about unclean lips.

In my opinion, the pair of seraphim’s wings symbolically (and the vision is full of symbols) are instructive for us – a pair of wings that cover the face – a symbol of love for God, a pair of wings that cover the body – a symbol of self-love, and a pair of wings that fly — a symbol of active love for others, neighbours, etc.

In the coals from the altar there is an image of the Sacrament of Communion, which is why these words are mentioned during Communion itself.

I would like to note that the answer to God’s question in Isaiah seems to burst out from his mouth. In my opinion, this shows that in God’s presence, even the secrets of the heart, thoughts, and personality traits become clear, which is why, for example, Peter on the Feast of Tabernacles speaks without fully realizing himself, and Isaiah in this vision.

It is difficult to understand the words about the judgment of God. In particular, about the numerical value.

And it is important to remember that in Isaiah this is a multi-faceted and multi-meaning concept. Here, in the vision, the glory of God is most visible, why one can see first of all those actions of God to stop sin, to destroy evil, which He does and will do.

There are words in the Scriptures that may well indicate that all people are descended from Adam and Eve bodily, but if you look deeper, this opinion is again not so unconditional.

Let’s look carefully.

Acts XVII, 26-29: And the whole human race He [that is, God] created from one [here in the male gender, that means, from Adam] to dwell on the entire surface of the earth, and appointed defined days [times, eras, epochs] and borders [boundaries] delaying them, so that they seek God, or they will not feel Him and find Him, even though He is not far from each of us. Because we live, and move, and exist in Him, just as some of your poets said: Even we are His kindred! Therefore, being of God’s kind, we should not think that the Godhead is like gold, or silver, or stone, a work of art or a human invention. Indeed, if we understand bodily and literally, then from Adam’s rib God created Eve, gave them children, protected them from diseases, settled peoples, giving them certain territories and times with their boundaries.

But from one in Greek, the preposition εκ is used, which means the source of action or movement (from where as a source? from what source?). But let’s look carefully: what action, what movement is meant?

If only what is in verses 26-27, i.e. physical displacement, the formation of peoples and nations, states, etc., the search for God, then the traditional understanding remains quite logical.

But if you keep in mind the entire quoted passage, including the words about the seed of God (that is, that people become children of God by grace, according to the promises and designs of God, His providence, while not being the fleshly offspring of God in any sense), and also mentioned yesterday at the link above considerations, also mentioned thoughts in the texts at the links:

then the answer to the question of the existence of other people is not so obvious.

In such a case, it seems possible that Adam is the bearer of the image of God, which in turn is mainly not in belonging to the biological species of man, but in spirituality and a relationship with God. And then the presence of other people, besides those mentioned, is possible.

Then Paul’s words would mean that people through Adam and Eve had received the image of God, had become truly human in the full sense, but together with Adam and Eve they also had received the damage of nature.

I am writing in a conditional way, because it looks hypothetical to me, and I am citing to be consistent in my reasoning.

In my opinion, taking into account hypothetical scenarios also to some extent allows us to better know God’s truth, which is what the Lord wants us to do.

God wants everyone to be saved and come to know the truth.
