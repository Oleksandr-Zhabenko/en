Liturgy:
(Romans V, 1-10)
Romans V, 1 — 'ἐκ πίστεως' — 'ek pisteos' - out of faith. It concerns becoming righteous through active manifestations of faith in life. See more at the links:
https://oleksandr-zhabenko.github.io/en/commentaries/20062026.html
https://oleksandr-zhabenko.github.io/en/commentaries/13062026.html
https://oleksandr-zhabenko.github.io/en/commentaries/10062026.html
https://oleksandr-zhabenko.github.io/en/commentaries/02062026.html
https://oleksandr-zhabenko.github.io/en/commentaries/22032025.html
'πρὸς τὸν θεὸν διὰ τοῦ κυρίου ἡμῶν Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ' — 'pros ton theon dia tou kyriou hemon Iesou CHristou' - towards God [in drawing near into God] through our Lord Jesus Christ. The preposition 'pros' here is with the accusative and indicates a drawing near into God in the closest personal relationships.

Romans V, 2 — 'προσαγωγὴν' — 'prosagogen' - an approach; access. 'εἰς τὴν χάριν ταύτην ἐν ᾗ ἑστήκαμεν' - into this grace in which we have been set; into this grace by which we have been set. That is, access into what, into Whom, and what it signifies.

Romans V, 3 — 'ἐν ταῖς θλίψεσιν' — 'en tais thlipsesin' - in the tribulations. That is, in what circumstances we boast.
Concerning the chain, hope, and love — a living Christian hope is a consequence of love from God, therefore when a man preserves and multiplies the gift of love, then they are enduring, and in them, in various life events and situations, is born a hope that makes not ashamed, [resting] upon God's love, eternal life, and victory over sins. This hope is not these gifts themselves, but upon the manifestation of all these gifts it becomes stronger.

Romans V, 5 — 'ἐν ταῖς καρδίαις ἡμῶν διὰ πνεύματος ἁγίου τοῦ δοθέντος ἡμῖν' — 'en tais kardiais hemon dia pneumatos hagiou tou dothentos hemin' - in the hearts of us through [the] Holy Spirit, the [One] having been given to us. That is, here it is not necessary to separate the Holy Spirit and God's love. The image of a fluid that flows, pours, fills, indicates the meaning of the comparison — the Holy Spirit fills in the heart ever new regions, places, like that fluid, and precisely in these regions is manifested God's love. The heart here is a place in which the Holy Spirit must overflow, flow further — to new places, so that there God's love might be manifested. One can here figuratively speak about the action of the Holy Spirit, God: the action of the Holy Spirit is a river, a current, a torrent of love that encompasses in the heart ever something new. See also:
https://www.facebook.com/Oleksandr.S.Zhabenko/posts/
Quoting:
Reflections on death for a benefactor, a righteous man, and a sinner — a benefactor evokes sincere feelings, in particular of gratitude, therefore for them one can more boldly die; a righteous man — cares how to please God, therefore among men does not evoke special admiration; and sinners — about them among men few think with love, not to speak already about suffering for them, and at the time when there were few Christians, almost no one thought [this way] except them.

End of quote.

Romans V, 8 — 'ἀπάγην εἰς ἡμᾶς' — 'agapen eis emas' - love into us. Shows the gradualness of the opening (the movement of revelation, manifestation) of God's love in Christ. Also indicates onto whom God's love leads His very Self — unto men, unto the search of them for their salvation.

Romans V, 9 — 'ἐν тῷ αἵματι αὐτοῦ' — 'en to haimati autou' - with His Blood. With what we become righteous, what leads men unto true righteousness, justification. 'ἀπὸ τῆς ὀργῆς' - 'apo tes orges' - away from the wrath. The preposition 'apo' indicates here that salvation signifies a complete deprivation of the state that evokes the righteous wrath of God, consequently, a complete becoming righteous.

Romans V, 10 — 'σωθησόμεθα ἐν τῇ ζωῇ αὐτοῦ' — 'sothesometha en te zoe autou' - we shall be saved in His life; we shall be saved with His life. Here both variants of translation are possible. The first indicates that the salvation of men is in the Resurrection of Christ out of the dead; the second — that the life of the Risen Christ is the salvation of men. We were reconciled — literally we were mutually changed, came to a compromise, exchanged. What is meant is that Christ died instead of us (an exchange), and this made men and God similar in Christ (brought close, became as if a compromise). The meaning of reconciliation is derivative out of the general sense of the passage. The Sacrifice of the Son of God became a Sacrifice of reconciliation.

(Matthew VI, 22-33)
Matthew VI, 22 — 'ἁπλοῦς' — 'haplous' - folded together, single, simple, perfect, complete (the eye). 'ὅλον' - 'holon' - all, whole. a folded together whole, all, single — presumably, in modern language — a full and integrated eye, that is, perception, awareness, vision, look, worldview; also the whole full body, that is, here rather the state of life, of human nature.

Matthew VI, 23 — 'ἐν σοὶ' — 'en soi' - in you. Where. The importance of the mind, perception, vision, attitude and the like in the life of a man, particularly the spiritual [life].

Matthew VI, 26 — 'ἐμβλέψατε εἰς τὰ πετεινὰ τοῦ οὐρανοῦ' — 'emblepsate eis ta peteina tou ouranou' - look closely into, well examine the birds (as if you live in their environment, among them, trying to be similar to them as your surroundings, neighbours) of the heaven. It concerns visible birds that have a body and bodily needs (a part of living nature). 'οὐδὲ συνάγουσιν εἰς ἀποθήκας' - 'oude synagousin eis apothekas' - neither do they gather together into barns (places for the storage of reserves). Where to.

Matthew VI, 27 — 'τίς δὲ ἐξ ὑμῶν' — 'tis de ex hymon' - and who out of you. That is, show Me such a man! Where is he? — as a manifestation of certainty that there is no such [person].

Matthew VI, 29 — 'οὐδὲ Σολομὼν ἐν πάσῃ τῇ δόξῃ αὐтоῦ περιεβάλετο ὡсь ἓν τούτων' — 'oude Solomon en pase te doxe autou periebaleto hos hen touton' - not [even] Solomon in all his glory was clothed like one of these (lilies). I wrote earlier, here I will bring a part of the quote:
Lest a thought should be formed out of the preceding that Christ disregards outward beauty, He speaks also this. Note that here the lilies turn out to be more glorious than Solomon, the chosen one of God, known for the fact that in his time there was peace and much gold, he built a temple and a very glorious temple for the Lord and was glorious not only outwardly, but also by his great wisdom. Here Christ unexpectedly says that a lily is more glorious than Solomon himself, God's chosen one. How so then, for this is completely opposite to that which He has just been saying, that you are more valuable than the grass. But the matter is in that beauty must be noticed; if one does not pay attention to the beauty around, the beauty of our very selves also, then it is unnoticeable often and it seems that even there is none of it. But as you begin to look closely, you will see what an abyss of this beauty is hidden where we, perhaps, also do not notice at all, for look: the lilies are beautiful by the fact that the Lord has put so much into them, in every cell, in every tissue of their organism, they are filled with God's design, God's care, and any researcher, a truly good botanist will say that very much is laid there, their organism – is truly wonderful. Concerning Solomon, it is spoken in the first place about his clothing: a man cares about clothing, creates it partially beautiful, one can say so, that they do not put into their clothing so much pre-eminence of wisdom as the Lord has laid into the lilies as an organism. Consequently, if we look upon this, then the lilies in this sense are more glorious, more beautiful than the clothing of Solomon.
Also here is noticeable a certain hierarchy of beauty: first things which we consider beautiful, more – living nature that does not have an immortal soul, in particular plants and animals (therefore completely Christian are the efforts of animal rights defenders, for example, regarding the matter not to use their fur for the sake of ornaments), still more – men who are created according to the image of God.
