Happy Synaxis of the 12 Apostles! Since yesterday was a great feast on a weekday, yesterday's readings have been transferred to today. I present them separately along with today's.

Liturgy:
(Romans XII, 4-5, 15-21)
Romans XII, 4 — 'ἐν ἑνὶ σώματι πολλὰ μέλη ἔχομεν' — 'en heni somati polla mele ekhomen' - in one body we have many members. Where.

Romans XII, 5 — 'ἐν Χριστῷ' — 'en CHristo' - in Christ. Indicates onto that being in Christ, the faithful become one, becoming a single whole, an integral mystical Church organism.

Romans XII, 16 — 'τὸ αὐτὸ εἰς ἀλλήλους φρονοῦν' — 'to auto eis allelous phronoun' - think the same thing into each other; think the same thing for each other. That is, like-mindedness is valuable not by itself, but for creating a community, a commonality. 'μὴ γίνεσθε φρόνιμοι παρ’ ἑαυτοῖς' - 'me ginesthe phronimoi par heautois' - do not become rich in thoughts in the vicinity of yourselves. 'par' before a vowel with the dative case here means in the vicinity of; near. The phrase signifies that there is no need for the faithful to compete over who among them is a more deeply-thinking person, or who is more cautious, circumspect, far-sighted, etc. For more on that, see the link:
https://www.facebook.com/Oleksandr.S.Zhabenko/posts/pfbid05PhidJjaBaxWEV3WJJUUeXCyScbNp5qXpD9i8Ha23ubCNbgB8X237Aq3sgVpNkVrl

Romans XII, 17 — 'προνοοмеνοι' — 'pronooumenoi' - thinking beforehand, provide. Important words for the building (creation) of peace.

Romans XII, 18 — 'εἰ δυνατόν, τὸ ἐξ ὑμῶν μετὰ πάντων ἀνθρώπων εἰρηνεύοντες' — 'ei dynaton to ex hymon meta panton anthropon eireneuontes' - if possible (if you are capable), this is from you, with all people live in peace. Here the preposition 'ex' (the form of 'ek' before a vowel) and the Greek word 'dynaton' indicate that peace must be a power and capability of Christians themselves. If a person rejects the Saviour, they can be placed in a state where they become a source of conflict, unable to be at peace. Conversely, those faithful to Christ can be at peace precisely thanks to union with Christ Himself. At the same time, these words in no way forbid fighting for peace when you or others are attacked, which, for example, the courageous defenders of Ukraine and Europe are doing right now. Another example is that Christians are forced to renounce their faith or a part of it for the sake of peace with unbelievers, then such proposals must be rejected and conquered completely. The faith of Christians does not depend on them, for it is granted by God. Therefore, one cannot renounce it. The faith of Christians is not from themselves, but from God.

Romans XII, 21 — 'μὴ νικῶ ὑπὸ τοῦ καкоῦ, ἀλλὰ νίκα ἐν τῷ ἀγαθῷ τὸ κακόν' — 'me niko hypo tou kakou, alla vika en to agatho to kakon' - be not overcome by evil (by what), but overcome in good (with good, by means of good) evil. The preposition 'hypo' here is used with the genitive case and indicates onto the passive voice of the participle be overcome (by what). Being good, overcome evil with goodness. Christians must not be a third party in the struggle between good and evil; it is impossible in a Christian way to overcome evil without being good, without being in good. There is no Christian victory over evil if it is not carried out and achieved in good.
These words are also highly relevant in connection with the war of the Russians against Ukraine. The violation and incomplete understanding of these words of the apostle today is one of the prerequisites for the emergence of the Russian world.

General instructions for Christian life.

Give place unto the wrath of God, — this actually is what a Christian needs to do with their own wrath if it is truly righteous. At the same time, it is necessary to fight against evil. In particular, in war it is necessary to stop evil (therefore a so-called defensive, just war is needed), but there is no need for a person to be emotionally angry themselves.

Regarding coals of fire upon the head — Paul literally quotes Proverbs XXV, 21-22. In the context of Paul's words, it concerns not to plague or to make it more painful for the enemy, but rather that the coals of fire upon the head name a real good, which a person, contrary to everything, begins to realize and see. Often a person can feel and hold dislike, prejudice, wrath, and enmity because they do not realize that someone is a Christian, or what that actually means. In such a case, when this person sees—or rather, deeply feels—love from the side of a Christian, they will fall into a state of deep emotional experience or even a shock, which is what is named coals. Once, it was precisely with a burning coal from under the altar that the Seraphim cleansed the lips of Isaiah. This is a cleansing coal, a coal of love, and Paul says it is better for a person to experience such a difficult and dramatic enlightenment than to continue holding onto evil, dislike, wrath, or the like. If such a person is humane, they will cease to be an enemy and can even become a friend (Paul himself is a vivid example); if not, they will at least try to change the relationship. If they do not do even this, then they are truly afflicted by sin, dominated and blinded by passions, and a still deeper enlightenment awaits them later. See also: 1 Corinthians XIV, 22-25.

(Matthew XII, 9-13)
Matthew XII, 9 — 'εἰς τὴν συναγωγὴν αὐτῶν' — 'eis ten synagogen auton' - into the synagogue of them. Where to.

Matthew XII, 11 — 'ἐξ ὑμῶν' — 'ex hymon' - of you. The preposition 'ek' in its form before a subsequent 'hy' indicates that that person is a representative of a community of people, meaning factually a person. 'εἰς βόθυνον' - 'eis bothynon' - into a pit. Where to. 'οὐχὶ κρατησει αὐτὸ καὶ ἐγερεῖ' - 'oukhi kratesei auto kai egerei' - will he not lay hold on it and lift [it] up. That is, in comparison with God, Who is also spoken of here, God manifests power and lifts up (a synonym for resurrects) the faithful.

Matthew XII, 13 — 'απεκαтеσταθη' — 'apekatestathe' - was restored to the original state (healthy).

The Lord teaches that for true good, time is always good.

(Romans XIV, 9-18 )
Romans XIV, 9 — 'εἰς τοῦτο' — 'eis touto' - for this. For what.

Romans XIV, 14 — 'ἐν κυρίῳ Ἰησοῦ' — 'en kyrio Iesou' - in the Lord Jesus. That is, Paul witnesses that this experience is from God, from Christ. 'οὐδὲν κοινὸν δι’ ἑαυτοῦ' - 'ouden koinon di heautou' - there is nothing unclean through itself. Being oneself in the true sense (in particular, existing consistently with one's nature) does not make a creation unclean. By nature, a creation is not unclean. But it can become such through what it does, or in connection with the fact that it acts as a temptation. That is, uncleanness is not a characteristic of nature or creation itself, but rather of a person's conscience regarding something. For example, food is not unclean, but without discernment and prudence, it can be made into an idol, or one can begin giving it a special spiritual meaning, etc. Therefore, the Lord marks it as clean and unclean for us. Let us recall also the rainbow, which the Lord marked as a sign of the covenant after the flood. See more about that at the link:
https://oleksandr-zhabenko.github.io/en/commentaries/17032026.html

Romans XIV, 15 — 'ὑπὲρ οὗ' — 'hyper hou' - for (for the sake of) whom.

Romans XIV, 17 — 'ἐν πνεύματι ἁγίῳ' — 'en pneumati hagio' - in the Holy Spirit. Here it is emphasized that the Holy Spirit fills the saved creation and, along with this, He grants everything else named here, as well as the unnamed.

Romans XIV, 18 — 'ἐν τούτῳ δουλεύων' — 'en touto douleuon' - in this serves; with (by means of) this serves. Here, according to meaning, it could be with this serves, but in this is a better translation that carries additional meaning. The point is that serves indicates the direction of the heart, of the personality, onto what a person does of their own for Christ. If one says with this serves, then it turns out that at least the gifts of the Holy Spirit, or even He Himself, become a method of a person's service, meaning the person as if appropriates God's gift to themselves. This, of course, is an incorrect understanding of the relationship between a person and God. Conversely, in this does not carry the meaning of appropriating the gifts of the Holy Spirit or Himself, but points to the driving power and fortification — Who and what prompts and establishes a person in this service to Christ. This latter is undoubtedly closer to the meaning of the words of the apostle Paul. Perhaps someone will want to object that a person cooperates with God in creating peace, righteousness, a holy life, and the like. But the gifts themselves come from God; in particular, a person cannot by their own forces attain the Kingdom of God, as it is always God's gift. Therefore, it is still illogical to think that with this serves is meant here, since this would precisely indicate that a person attains the Kingdom of God by their own forces, rather than receiving it as a gift of God.

(Matthew XII, 14-16, 22-30)
Matthew XII, 24 — 'μὴ ἐν τῷ Βεελζεβοὺλ' — 'me en to Beelzeboul' - not by Beelzebub. Here, contrary to what was written above regarding Romans XIV, 18, it is more expedient to use the instrumental case, i.e., with whom? with what?. For a person who acts together with demons, it is not necessary to be properly in one of them or to be possessed by him, although this is also possible. Jesus does not act with demons, which He speaks of right here, combatting this accusation.

Matthew XII, 26 — 'εμερισθη' — 'emeristhe' - was divided into parts — from 'μεριζω' - 'merizo' - to divide into parts.

I wrote earlier, to quote:
To conquer an external challenge, internal unity is needed. For an external threat comes onto internal territory, and if that territory has forces that are directed against each other—forces approximately equal and comparable — then external challenges will be able to overcome the resistance. On the contrary, otherwise an external threat on internal territory will not have enough forces for victory. A very important thought at the present time for Ukraine and Europe.

End of quote.

Matthew XII, 27 — 'ἐν Βεελζεβοὺλ' — 'en Beelzeboul' - with Beelzebub — see above. 'ἐν τίνι' - 'en tini' - with whom.

Matthew XII, 28 — 'ἐν πνεύματι θεοῦ' — 'en pneumati theou' - in the Spirit of God; with the Spirit of God. Most translations give the second variant. The choice affects understanding, since in the first case Jesus speaks of His human nature, comparing with the children of the Pharisees, who are also people. The human nature of Jesus is divinised, filled with the Holy Spirit, which is why Jesus also has the name Christ, that is, Anointed One. In contrast to the sons of the Pharisees, who cast out demons by unclear means and authority, Jesus can from a theological perspective cast out demons by operating in particular through His human nature (which must one way or another participate in His action as God according to the dogma of the 6th Ecumenical Council), though then properly the source of power and authority is the Holy Spirit. Also, in this case, it is necessary to remember that the human nature of Jesus is inseparably united with the Divine nature in the single Hypostasis of the Incarnate Son of God; therefore, it acts accordingly, knowing, as the fathers say, that it is the human nature of the Son of God, to Whom everything is subjected as God. On the other hand, the second variant of translation is also completely logical and concerns more the Divine nature of Jesus, because it emphasizes His Divine dignity. A comparison with the parallel passage in Luke (Luke XI, 20), where the expression with the finger of God is used, points rather to the second variant than to the first, because it is difficult to think of the human nature of Jesus in the finger of God. Considering what has been said, I am also inclined to think that the second variant is more appropriate here.

Matthew XII, 29 — 'εἰς τὴν οἰκίαν' — 'eis ten oikian' - into (inside) the house. Where to.
