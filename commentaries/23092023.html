I would also add that seeking one’s own is synonymous with being closed off and self-focused.

We can also say that the Apostle Paul teaches a wise attitude towards all of creation.

If a person thinks: I can do this and that, so why not try it?, then they can easily substitute a need, something that is really necessary for them, for their salvation, for eternal life (and often for temporary life), something that is really necessary in the same sense for other people, with apparent opportunity and freedom. The Apostle teaches us to seek what is truly needed.
