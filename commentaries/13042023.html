(John XIII, 3-17)

Jesus loved to the end those faithful who are in the world – at the time when the apostles showed their greatest weakness, greatest misunderstanding, the Lord, on the contrary, gave them almost the greatest good. Until the end – that is, he loved in the greatest weakness with a single, whole, saving love – not in the sense that he had not loved like that before, but in the fact that it manifested and made itself the most evident at this time. The Lord, as God, already knew all the details of these events, all the weakness shown by the disciples, but then His love was not so obvious, but at this time it became the most obvious.
