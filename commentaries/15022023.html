Today is the Feast of the Candlemas, the Meeting of the Lord, the only one of the 12 largest, dedicated both to Christ and to the Mother of God. Accordingly, the Gospel was read today with a story about the event
(Luke II, 22-40)
The event took place on the 40th day after the birth of Jesus, which is why it is celebrated according to the calendar in this way, completing the Christmas cycle of holidays. Today also caroling is finished.

As the days of purification passed – that is, for a woman who gave birth to a boy and to the boy himself, it was 40 days, after which they could enter the temple. Jesus does not needed the purification, similarly the Immaculate Virgin was sanctified by the birth of Jesus, She did not need purification, but She fulfilled the Law. For all of us, this is an example that there is nothing in vain in God's will.

It was promised by the Holy Spirit that he would not see death before he should see the promised Messiah (Christ) – according to the famous Tradition, Simeon was one of the 72 translators who performed the first translation of the Old Testament from Hebrew into Greek – the so-called Translation of the Seventy (Seventy-Two), in Greek – the Septuagint. The translation was recognized as divinely inspired no less than the original, it was widely used by the Jews in the dispersion, in particular, at the time of the birth of Jesus. This translation took place in the 3rd century. to the Christmas of Christ, therefore, Simeon himself was about 300 years old at the time of the event of today's celebration – a unique example of longevity from the time of the most ancient people. The same Tradition clarifies that he was translating the book of the prophet Isaiah, and when he came to the place where it is said: Behold, a Virgin will conceive in the womb and give birth to a Son…, then he doubted how a virgin could conceive while remaining a virgin, wanted to correct the young woman, and suddenly an Angel appeared to Simeon and announced that the Scripture is correct, not mistaken, and he will really see the fulfillment of these words himself, before he dies. You can see from this how long he waited, and with what joy he met the Lord and the Holy Family in the Lord's temple.

Simeon's song was included in the service, and is sung before the beginning of the morning service (which is served the evening before).

Simeon's prophecy is the first testimony about the future suffering of Jesus (and with Him, also spiritually, His Mother) and the life of the Church in the New Testament. In fact, in his words we meet the whole truth about Christ, and we see with what humility, without doubts, disputes or unbelief, the Immaculate Virgin accepts the heralded Revelation. This place is important in order to see Her long-suffering as well – She is greater than the ancient Job the Long-suffering, who said in his suffering: Here I have received good from God, now I must be patient, accepting suffering, may the Lord's name be blessed!.
