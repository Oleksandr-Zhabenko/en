Liturgy:
Of Co-Apostolic Prince Volodymyr of Kyiv, in holy Baptism Basil:
(Galatians I, 11-19)
Galatians I, 11 — 'τὸ εὐαγγέλιον τὸ εὐαγγελισθὲν ὑπ’ ἐμοῦ' — 'to euangelion to euangelisthen hyp emou' - the Gospel preached by me. The preposition 'hypo' in its form before a subsequent vowel here is with the genitive case and indicates the passive voice of the participle preached and who preached it — Paul.

Galatians I, 12 — 'παρὰ ἀνθρώπου' — 'para anthropou' - from beside human. The preposition 'para' here is with the genitive case. Such a construction is used regarding the procession of the Holy Spirit from the Father, regarding the Most Holy Trinity, regarding the whole God's design, and regarding the human nature of Jesus Christ. See more at the links:
https://oleksandr-zhabenko.github.io/en/commentaries/02082025.html
https://oleksandr-zhabenko.github.io/uk/commentaries/Pryjmennyky.html
https://oleksandr-zhabenko.github.io/en/commentaries/28052026.html
Here, since there is a negation, it means that the apostle Paul did not learn from some human, as many other preachers often do, learning and receiving knowledge from people who teach under the action of the Holy Spirit for the glorification of the Incarnate Son of God, but Paul received the gift of preaching through direct Revelations of the Lord Jesus Christ. It emphasizes the importance of preaching and teaching, but at the same time, the exceptional value of the apostolic ministry.

Galatians I, 13, 14 — 'ἐν τῷ Ἰουδαϊσμῷ' — 'en to Ioudaismo' - in Judaism. That is, in what faith and tradition.

Galatians I, 14 — 'ἐν τῷ γένει μου' — 'en to genei mou' - in my nation; among my people. Where, there is also a certain comparison here with the environment and relatives.

Galatians I, 15 — 'ἐκ κοιλίας μητρός μου' — 'ek koilias metros mou' - from my mother's womb. The preposition 'ek' here indicates origin, not time. That is, the constancy of God's choosing is emphasized, rather than a certain moment in time. See also similar at the links:
https://oleksandr-zhabenko.github.io/en/commentaries/17052026.html
https://oleksandr-zhabenko.github.io/en/commentaries/16052026.html

Galatians I, 16 — 'ἐν ἐμοὶ' — 'en emoi' - in me; by me. Both variants of translation are possible: the first indicates that Paul represents Christ by God's will, and the second — that Paul, as a servant of God (more precisely, as an instrument in God's hands), reveals Christ to others. 'ἐν τοῖς ἔθνεσιν' - 'en tois ethnesin' - in the Gentiles; among the Gentiles; in the nations. All translation options are possible, since the word is polysemantic.

Galatians I, 17, 18 — 'εἰς Ἱεροσόλυμα' — 'eis Hierosolyma' - into Jerusalem. Where to. 'εἰς Ἀραβίαν' - 'eis Arabian' - into Arabia. Where to. 'εἰς Δαμασκόν' - 'eis Damaskon' - into Damascus. Where to.

The story of Paul's conversion, in particular, important details regarding his first steps as a Christian. Holy Prince Volodymyr is often compared to the apostle Paul, since Volodymyr, before accepting Christ, was not a model of virtue, but with His acceptance, he changed. And the general baptism of the Rus people was of immense importance for the spread of the faith and the building of the state.

Paul emphasizes that apostleship originates not from people, but from God; no one can by human will, even of entire nations, lead an entire nation to Christ, as the apostles did, therefore the calling to apostleship is exclusively and only from God.
