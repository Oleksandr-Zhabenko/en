2) James 4:11-12 – seems illogical at first glance: the Law of Moses spells out what is sin, and if it is used to expose sin, then it is used as intended. But then why does such person judge the Law, is the judge, not the doer? I will highlight two reasons (you also need to take into account what was written on January 13, 2023 on my Facebook page here

https://www.facebook.com/Oleksandr.S.Zhabenko/posts/pfbid02wx2z6mA1kZ71sie5GvVUmNgzLh5i8bVRQXygQyps9Nyr6458k4wsXrd8XzbQMBL4l

):

a) The law is designed to judge sin, not people, that is, it provides not only judgment for sinners, but also protection. Therefore, a person who considers the Law only as a list of possible violations and punishments (like the Criminal code) and judging his / her neighbour, such a person easily violates the protective part of the Law due to his / her weakness and sinfulness. Therefore, judges the Law.

b) doer of the Law â€” used derivative of the word to create (like writing poems); performer therefore implies a creative approach to the Law, not just a mechanical literal performance, but a creative process, such as performing a song, reciting and writing poetry or music, etc. Fulfilling the Law (similarly also the Gospel) means creatively (poetically) fulfilling it in one's own life and in the lives of others. Does not fulfill the Law (Gospel) (does not create the Law (Gospel)) and a person who does not have this co-creation with God. A person who judges his neighbors as a judge on the basis of the Law does not have full creative cooperation with God, who wants to save people. And this partiality, according to the beginning of the 2nd chapter of the message, is a violation of everything.
