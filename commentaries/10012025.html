(Mark VI, 45-53)
Mark VI, 45 — ‘εἰς τὸ πλοῖον’ - ‘eis to ploion’ - ‘into the boat (the same one mentioned earlier)’. ‘εἰς τὸ πέραν πρὸς Βηθσαϊδάν’ - ‘eis to peran pros Bethsaidan’ - ‘to the other side to Bethsaida’. The preposition ‘eis’ is part of a common expression here.

Mark VI, 46 — ‘εἰς τὸ ὄρος’ - ‘eis to oros’ - ‘to the mountain; onto the mountain’.

Mark VI, 47 — ‘ἐν μέσῳ τῆς θαλάσσης’ - ‘en meso tes thalasses’ - ‘in the middle of the sea; in the midst of the sea’.

Mark VI, 48 — ‘ἐν τῷ ἐλαύνειν’ - ‘en to elaunein’ - ’while rowing’.

Mark VI, 51 — ‘εἰς τὸ πλοῖον’ - ‘eis to ploion’ - ‘into the boat’. ‘λίαν ἐκ περισσοῦ’ - ‘lian ek perissou’ - ‘exceedingly in abundance'. ‘ἐν ἑαυτοῖς’ - ‘en heautois’ - ‘in themselves’.

Mark VI, 53 — ‘εἰς Γεννησαρὲτ’ - ‘eis Gennesaret’ - ‘to Gennesaret’.
