(Romanians IX, 18-33; Romanians X, 11 — XI, 2)
Romans IX, 21 — ‘ἐκ τοῦ αὐτοῦ φυράματος ποιῆσαι ὃ μὲν εἰς τιμὴν σκεῦος, ὃ δὲ εἰς ἀτιμίαν' - ek tou autou phyramatos poiesai o men eis timen skeuos o de eis atimian - from the same mass to create either a vessel in honour (as a destination of movement) or in dishonour.

Romans IX, 24 — ‘ἡμᾶς’ - ‘emas’ - ‘us’. ‘ἐξ Ἰουδαίων ἀλλὰ καὶ ἐξ ἐθνῶν’ - ‘ex ioudaion alla kai ex ethnon’ - ‘from Jews and from Gentiles’.

Romans IX, 29 — ‘ἡμῖν’ - ‘emin’ - ‘to us’.

Romans IX, 30, 32 — ‘ἐκ πίστεως’ - ‘ek pisteos’ - ‘from faith’ (where it comes from). ‘ἐξ ἔργων’ - ‘ek ergon’ - ‘from works’.

Romans IX, 31 — ‘Ἰσραὴλ δὲ διώκων νόμον δικαιοσύνης εἰς νόμον οὐκ ἔφθασεν’ - ‘israel de diokon nomon dikaiosynes eis nomon ouk ephthasen’ - literally: ‘But Israel, who sought the law, did not attain righteousness in the law’. This translation is also more logical than many others, which include ‘law of righteousness’ alongside ‘law’. The problem is not that the ‘law of righteousness’ is unattainable (what is it, anyway? Where else does Paul speak of it?), but that righteousness remained unattainable after the law apart from Christ.

Romans X, 12 — ‘πλουτῶν εἰς πάντας’ - ‘plouton eis pantas’ - ‘rich in all’. It indicates the gradual manifestation, revelation, and revealing of God's generosity in people.

Romans X, 16 — ‘ἡμῶν’ - ‘emon’ - ‘us’.

Romans X, 18 — ‘εἰς πᾶσαν τὴν γῆν’ - ‘eis pasan ten gen’ - ‘to the whole earth’. ‘εἰς τὰ πέρατα τῆς οἰκουμένης’ - ‘eis ta perata tes oikoumenes’ - ‘to the end of the world’ (the world then known to be inhabited by people).

(Matthew XI, 2-15; Matthew XI, 16-20)
Matthew XI, 7 — ‘ὑπὸ ἀνέμου’ - ‘(h)ypo anemou’ - ‘under the wind’.

Matthew XI, 12 — ‘ἀπὸ δὲ τῶν ἡμερῶν Ἰωάννου’ - ‘apo de ton emeron ioannou’ - ‘from the days of John’.

Matthew XI, 19 — ‘ἀπὸ τῶν ἔργων’ - ‘apo ton ergon’ - ‘from the works’.
