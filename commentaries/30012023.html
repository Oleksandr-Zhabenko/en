There were 2 readings today, one of which was for St. Anthony the Great.

(Mark XII, 13-17)

From the daily Gospel we read about Caesar's give to Caesar, and God's -– to God in Mark, we recently analyzed the text.

(1 Peter II, 21 – III, 9)

From the daily Apostle it was read:

1) about patience in suffering for Christ, and about the feat of Jesus Himself for our salvation,

2) about the relationship between husbands and wives.

1) called to that – not called to suffering, but to loyalty to God, to love that even suffering cannot break, destroy, violate. But if someone does not have Jesus in front of their spiritual eyes, then for such a person, although it is possible to recognize a possible feat of patience for the sake of good, it is difficult to have real firmness and faithfulness in this, because you were like those sheep that wandered.

2) a voluminous topic, we partially touched on it earlier, considering similar thoughts of Paul. Due to lack of space, I will not dwell on everything in detail, I will note that Peter emphasizes the joint-heiring of women and men in grace (and implicitly – the equality and equal dignity of both sexes).

there were no obstacles to your (joint) prayers – hence the rule that one should live in such a way that it does not harm prayer, in particular joint prayer. That which interferes with prayer, which cannot be directed in the direction of God, should be bypassed, or even we should struggle against it. Each person judges (thinks, reason) about this most often according to conscience, therefore it is desirable that husbands and wives have commonality, similarity.

1 Peter III, 1 – were captured by the behavior of the wife – rather, certainly, overcomed, i.e. first of all impressed (not necessarily emotionally, but significantly), i.e. the behavior must be glorious according to the biblical understanding of glory.
