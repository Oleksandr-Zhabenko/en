(Isaiah LXV, 8-16)

Isaiah, as an Old Testament man, prophesies about God’s judgment and reward for the faithful.

Isaiah LXV, 10 – valley – ‘emeq’ (pronounced like ‘emek’) – a place where you can renew your strength (moving in a mountainous area requires effort, and a valley is a place where you can preserve and multiply, increase, renew — an image understood by shepherds and residents of areas where there is no plain).

To leave the name is to lose power, that is, in the Kingdom of God evil people will lose all power.

Swear by name is here synonymous with power (people swore as a sign of certainty, and therefore of the power of their words). That is, in the Kingdom of God, the faithful will have strength from God.

(Proverbs XXIII, 15 – XXIV, 5)

Instructions are more widespread than many previous ones, mainly against drunkenness, addiction to wine, adultery, also against indulging in various passions, laziness. A call to listen to the advice and guidance, especially elders (especially parents), wisdom, mastery and decency.

Mother is ‘em’, truth, faithfulness, truth is ‘emeth’ (‘eh-meth’).

(Ezekiel XLIII, 27 – XLIV, 4)

The prophecy about the New Testament, and then – about the Incarnation of the Son of God and the virginity of the Mother of God – before Christmas, at Christmas and after Christmas - in the image of a gate through which only the Lord, not a man, passed.

The glory of the Lord here is the glory (gifted to) of the Mother of God, who is higher than the angels.
