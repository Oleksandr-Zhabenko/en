Roman Catholics today have Ash Wednesday, Lent began (this year until April 9), For the Greek Orthodox Christians, the last preparatory week for fasting continues. Easter for the Orthodox and Greek Catholics this year is on April 16.

From now on, services are served as in the time of Lent. On Monday Lent begins. During weekdays of fasting (which are included in the number of 40 days of multi-day fasting) there are no liturgical readings from the New Testament, but psalms and Old Testament books are read, including the prophetic, teaching, and book of Genesis.

(Joel II, 12-26, III, 12-21)

Today there were no New Testament readings, but there was a reading from the book of the prophet Joel, one of the 12 little prophets. The prophet calls to fasting, remembering the Israelites of God mercy. In the time of the prophet Joel, the Gospel word was not yet heard, so the Israelites often fasted in sorrow. Fasting in its own sense was a time of spiritual focus, for the sake of restoration, improvement of relations with God, for the sake of purification, a preparatory time. Fasting was a kind of impulse, impetus for renewal the relationship with God. Over time, the Pharisees began to perceive fasting in a completely different way, for which they were exposed by Christ. And now Joel calls precisely to that inner, heartfelt impulse.

The second thing Joel says is that the Lord by His actions will lead to the successful fulfillment of His will and give blessings.

Perhaps it is advisable to try to ensure that the voice can be clearly associated with oneself (I).
