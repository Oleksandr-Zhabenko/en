Glory be to Jesus Christ!

(Luke XXII, 48) — "Judas, do you give out the Son of Man with a kiss?", — the question of God the Creator to man, which shows that God created kisses for love, closeness (intimacy), special manifestation of personality, when through the body is also revealed something that is not reduced only to the body. The kiss is created by God by design as a sign, that is, it always means something, points to (probably better to translate, indicates) something, but Judas distorted himself in a way that commits a serious sin by kissing as a sign of his betrayal, a sign of the evil one. A kiss may point to (indicate, be related to) the body, may point to the soul, may — to the evil one (as in the case of Judas' kiss), or may — to God. The Lord reveals the depth of Judas' fall by calling him to repentance through manifestation of his sin (oh, if Judas had really thought about the words of Christ, he could repent, instead he died in despair afterwards).

Lord, grant us mercy to be faithful!

Glory be to You, our God, glory be to You!
