Dedicated to Sophie Kok, the sister of Emma Kok, today is Sophie's 19th Birthday (she is 18).

Sophie Kok studies international and European law. It occurred to me that one of my best pieces of writing has to do with the law.

In On the existence of God and testimony of Him, it is proven that the existence of God cannot be strictly proved, just as it is impossible to strictly prove the non-existence of God. In international and European law, the presumption of innocence provides that a person is presumed innocent until proven guilty. Since it is impossible to prove the existence or non-existence of God in a strict manner, all people who are tried as criminals by anyone in the international community because of their belief or non-belief in God cannot be considered guilty, their guilt cannot be proven, and therefore they must be considered innocent in the eyes of the law and set free if they are under restraint or other oppression.

God created the world so that people can believe, not to force them to believe in Him.

Glory be to Thee, our God, glory be to Thee!
